Sachin Tendulkar

God of Cricket-"The little master blaster"

Timeline of Sachin Tendulkar's major achievements:-

  • 1989: A 16-year-old Tendulkar makes his Test debut against Pakistan
  • 1990: Tendulkar notches up his maiden Test century
  • 1994: Scores his first ODI hundred in his 79th match
  • 1996: Piles up 523 runs, including two centuries, in the World Cup hosted by India, Pakistan and Sri Lanka.
  • 1997: Wins the Wisden cricketer of the year award.
  • 2001: Becomes the first batsman in the history of ODI cricket to score 10,000 runs.
  • 2002: In his 99th Test appearance, surpasses Sir Donald Bradman's record of 29 centuries
  • 2003: Makes 673 runs in 11 matches in the ICC World Cup -- most by any batsman in a single World Cup and wins the player of the tournament award.
  • 2004: Equals Sunil Gavaskar's world record 34 Test centuries. Becomes only the third player in history, after Gary Kirsten and Steve Waugh, to score centuries against all Test playing nations. Also becomes the first player in ODI history to win 50 Man of the Match awards.
  • 2005: Completes 10,000 runs in Test cricket in his 122nd match.
  • 2006: Sets a new world record by completing 14,000 one-day runs
  • 2008: Tendulkar becomes the first batsman to go past 16,000 runs in ODIs. Surpassing Brian Lara's tally of 11953 runs to become the highest run-scorer in Test cricket.
  • 2009: Becomes the first batsman to aggregate 17,000 ODI runs during his innings of 175 against Australia at Hyderabad.
  • 2010: Becomes the first batsman to score an ODI double-century.
  • 2011: Plays a big role in India's World Cup triumph. He was the highest run-getter for India with 482 runs.
  • 2012: Scores his 100th international century in the Asia Cup match against Bangladesh. He scored 114.
  • 2012: Announces retirement from one-day international cricket.
  • 2013: Announces he will retire from Test cricket after playing in his 200th Test against the West Indies.
